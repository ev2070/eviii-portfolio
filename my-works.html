U.S. History Study Guide

by Elizabeth Vazquez and Nadia Ousman

This website was made for high school juniors to study in preparation for U.S. History exams and projects. The website discusses ten units of U.S. history in order for users from NYC high school curriculum.

At the time of development, my collaborator and good friend, Nadia Ousman, and I were taking a U.S. history class. We were fairly confident in our knowledge on the subject and wanted to help our student body better their understanding.

We tried to keep the information in this guide as accurate and concise as possible, made use of collapsing and expanding headers, and combined it with a simple and intuitive design to avoid overwhelming users.

We have since shown the website to U.S. history instructors who showed their eagerness to use it as a resource for future cohorts.

We also showcased this website winning us the title of High School of Telecommunication Arts and Technology Third Annual Software Engineering Program Exposition Finalist.

vaXine

by Elizabeth Vazquez and Laura Arias

This website served as a public service announcement for a Quality of Life Innovations proposal. My collaborator and other good friend, Laura Arias, and I were researching the question:

How does information about the effects of vaccinations affect New York City residents’ willingness to vaccinate their children?

In this website, we tried to provide basic information about vaccines and other useful resources. It also contains our field research and an online petition to implement vaccine curriculum in NYC pubilc schools to combat vaccine misinformation.

Our research project (including this website) were submitted into the City-Wide Quality of Life Innovations Competition Sponsored by the Wise Foundation and we were awarded the honors of Borough Leaders and Semi-Finalists.

Skydance Hot Air Balloon Ride

by Elizabeth Vazquez and Felix Zhu

This website was a made as a task for my collaborater, Felix Zhu, and I to make. We were given the roles of website designers and our client requested we make a website for Skydance Hot Air Balloon Ride (SHABR).

We were provided with the website's content and took off. We established hierarchy, kept a consistent color scheme, tinkered with fonts.

Our proudest accomplishment was the gallery of images we were given. We organized the images to be presented as an automatic slideshow to give the website a little bit of life.

The feedback we received was great because upperclassmen had a chance to decide which designs were their favorites. Some changes I would make would be tweaking font sizes here and there and using brighter colors as the current ones are quite muted.

My GitHub Tutorial

by Elizabeth Vazquez

I made this website with the intention of helping other students or beginners learn about Git and GitHub and their distinctions from one another.

I know when I first started learning how to use the two, I was beyond confused. I thought there was no hope for me. So to assist with the learning curves others may experience, I decided to make this page.

I only used markdown to make the information I provided the star of the show.

The page is around four years old, so some of its content may be outdated. For example, I mention cloud9 quite a bit because at the time, that was the IDE my peers and I were using.

Other than that, the information I provide should be spot on as I continue to refer to this page to remind myself of certain git commands.

Pong Remix

by Elizabeth Vazquez

Just another pong game. This was a fun, simple project to create.

To start playing, simply tap on the game and press the SPACE key. If you begin to feel overwhelmed, no problem. Press the SPACE key to pause the game or resume it.

Never played pong before? The objective is straightforward. Hit the ball with the paddle for as long as possible.

My version of the game adds elements of challenge. You can't see your cursor. The paddle shrinks in length everytime the ball hits it. And everytime the ball hits the paddle or the bottom of the screen, it's speed will increase.

I was in a generous mood when I decided to give players five lives. Pong, being the classic that it is, inspired me to use Nintendo Super Mario Bros pixelated hearts to represent that.

There is no official high score to chase after. The game will end, however, once all five lives have been lost. Enjoy!

Tail on the Turkey

by Elizabeth Vazquez and Aaliyah Sealey

My collaborator and friend, Aaliyah Sealey, and I were inspired by the spirit of Thanksgiving to make this game.

The rules are: tap inside the turkey's cirle and try to get eight points to wine.

I bet $100, in good faith, you can't beat this game.

This game is extremely challenging. There is only one turkey in the bunch and each time you successfully click inside its circle, the birds will start moving faster.

It's a game of precision. The birds were intentionally made into silhouttes to confuse players.

Another challenge is the timing involved to gain points. When we say click inside the turkey's circle, we mean release your mouse click to get the point as opposed to getting points on click. And if you misclick, that's too bad. The speed of the birds is merciless because they'll never slow down.

The highest score to date is 4 points. Have fun!

sueño

by Elizabeth Vazquez

For this project, I made an imaginary company called sueño. In Spanish, sueño means dream, sleep, and sleepiness.

This imaginary company provides a variety of quality beds from the good ol' mattress to a hammock and everything in between. Smaller products and accessories like neck pillows and sleep masks are also available.

sueño is a company that prioritizes its clients who struggle to get proper sleep. They use machine learning and collaborate with medical professionals to have their products tailored to each customer.

Their slogan: Comfort and health is a dream we make come true.

I made three drafts of sueño's logos. I wanted to convey a pillowy, comfortable feeling that sueño would like to portray. I used different combinations of fonts, visual symbols (e.g. the moon, sleeping figures, stars, etc.), color, and orientation. My final draft is of a sleeping cat on a cushion with the moon being alluded. The circles and sleeping eyes are a motif I kept throughout the design process.

Sunshine Deli

by Elizabeth Vazquez

This is another project in which I created an imaginary business called Sunshine Deli.

Sunshine Deli has a menu with nine different food sections: Breakfast, Bagels, Wraps, Burritos, Burgers, Sides & Desserts, Sandwiches, Salads, and Beverages.

The deli boasts a total of forty-two food items. You cannot go wrong with this deli. Just take a look at those prices!

Sunshine Deli is inspired by Brooklyn delis and its food items suit the tastes of local residents of Sunset Park—from which the name derives.

There are two different versions of their menu: print and screen. The print menu is the traditional trifold deli menu. The screen menu extracts the contents from the print version and reorganizes it into an intuitive mobile experience. You can interact with the screen menu on Figma. The illustrations of the food items were also my creations.

Queen - Text Texture

by Elizabeth Vazquez

This a quick exercise that explores text texture.

I used the famous band Queen for inspiration to the content.

From the get go, viewers can see lyrics from their hit song "We are the Champions."

Different typefaces were used in the efforts to effectively communicate the lyrics as they were sung. For example, I had the chorus be in all caps and spaced out to show how grand that lyric is. I took a lyric about going on and on and made associated it with a running circle. I had a more poetic verse look justified to the right to make it look like a poem. And I had the background text be contents from a Wikipedia page because it's all background information about Queen.

And I couldn't help but frame this masterpiece with a border.

Neapolitan - Graphic Treatment

by Elizabeth Vazquez

Here are some graphic treatments I made inspired by neapolitan ice cream. The colors of strawberry, vanilla, and chocolate are very appealing to me and having them paired with a soft shades of blue is like the cherry on top of a sundae.

The color scheme, images, and typefaces remained contant throughout the treatments, but playing around with the placement, orientation, and hierarchy of all the elements was key in portraying different aesthetics of the same concept.

The two different typefaces that were utilized complement each other nicely by adding visual contrast.

Some other subtle differences are the cropping of the images as texture, the change in color sequence to play around with unity, and rotated grids.

Owl - Image Trace

by Elizabeth Vazquez

In this work, I took a screenshot of "The Garden of Earthly Delights," a highly detailed painting, and I focused on a small owl. The owl is perched inside an ethereal structure erected from a body of water looking outside.

I start off by making lineart for the owl with the Adobe Illustrator pen tool. Then I vectorize the original image into a high fidelity photograph. Then I add myself into the image by using the owl's eye as a motif and having it appear like it's perched on a bush of zany roses. I really wanted to push for the insanity I found while looking at the owl.

I then took the original image to Photoshop. I made three versions of the owl again. The first had a halftone-like filter placed over it. The second had the background of the ethereal structure removed. And the last one was another ludicrous design where I made the owl look caught in somewhat of a motion blur and inverted some colors. Now it looks like the owl is stuck in an egg.

Jacobs Building Redesign

by Elizabeth Vazquez, Alex Duan, and Malek Gallichio

All NYU Tandon freshmen have to take the EG-UY 1003 Introduction to Engineering and Design course. In that course, freshmen work on a semester long project of their choosing. The one I chose alongside my other group members, Alex Duan and Malek Gallichio, was the Housing & Innovation in Revit (HIR) project.

The objective was to redesign the old Jacobs Building into a college campus expansion for NYU Tandon. We were given specifications about the property and required facilities to include. We were also asked to use our schematics to deduce cost of operation and figure out ways to have the campus expansion be LEED accredited.

Our group name was MAE Enterprise and our slogan is: You MAE rest assured when MAE is on the job!

I was in charge of handling the majority of designs that went into this project. I made preliminary sketches and built the building on Autodesk Revit by myself. I also made our group's logo.
