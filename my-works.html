U.S. History Study Guide

by Elizabeth Vazquez and Nadia Ousman

This is a study guide for high school juniors to study in preparation for U.S. History exams and projects. Ten units of U.S. history from New York City's high school curriculum are discussed.

My collaborator, Nadia Ousman, and I have shown the website to U.S. history instructors who showed their eagerness to use it as a resource for future cohorts.

We also showcased this website winning us the title of High School of Telecommunication Arts and Technology Third Annual Software Engineering Program Exposition Finalist.

vaXine

by Elizabeth Vazquez and Laura Arias

This is a public service announcement to combat vaccine misinformation and is targeted towards NYC public schools. My collaborator, Laura Arias, and I researched the question:

How does information about the effects of vaccinations affect New York City residents’ willingness to vaccinate their children?

Our research project - including this website - were submitted into the City-Wide Quality of Life Innovations Competition Sponsored by the Wise Foundation and we were awarded the honors of Borough Leaders and Semi-Finalists.

My GitHub Tutorial

by Elizabeth Vazquez

This is a tutorial to help students and beginners get started with Git, GitHub, and their distinctions from one another.

After all, it's very frequent that beginners struggle with the learning curve that these topics evoke.

The tutorial is around four years old, so some of its content may be outdated. For example, cloud9 is mentioned, but that is an obsolete IDE.

All other information should be spot on and is something I continue to refer to as a Computer Science major.

Pong Remix

by Elizabeth Vazquez

This is a remix of the classic pong game made for desktop.

To start playing, click inside the game and press the SPACE key. Press SPACE again to pause and resume the game.

Hit the ball with the paddle for as long as possible. Each successful hit or miss will shrink the paddle and increase the ball's velocity. There are five lives, but there is no official high score to chase after.

Enjoy!

Tail on the Turkey

by Elizabeth Vazquez and Aaliyah Sealey

This game is inspired by the spirit of Thanksgiving.

Tap inside the turkey's circle and aim for eight points to win. Each successful click inside the turkey's circle, will have the birds moving faster and they will never slow down.

It's a game of precision.

I bet $100, in good faith, you can't beat this game. The highest score to date is 4 points. Have fun!

sueño

by Elizabeth Vazquez

These are the logos I created for an imaginary company called sueño. In Spanish, sueño means dream, sleep, and sleepiness.

sueño provides a variety of quality beds from the good ol' mattress to a hammock and everything in between. Smaller products and accessories like neck pillows and sleep masks are also available.

Their slogan: Comfort and health is a dream we make come true.

These logos convey a pillowy, comfortable feeling that a company like sueño would like to get across.

Sunshine Deli

by Elizabeth Vazquez

These are menus for an imaginary business called Sunshine Deli. Their menu boasts nine different food sections and a total of forty-two food items.

There are two versions of the menu: print and screen. The print menu is a traditional trifold menu, and the screen menu provides an intuitive mobile experience. You can interact with the screen menu on Figma. The illustrations of the food items were also my creations.

Sunshine Deil is inspired by Brooklyn delis and its name derives from the Sunset Park neighborhood.

Queen - Text Texture

by Elizabeth Vazquez

This is an exercise that explores text texture and takes inspiration from the famous band Queen.

Lyrics from Queen's hit song "We are the Champions" was used with different typefaces and compositions to effectively communicate the lyrics as they were sung.

From the get go, viewers can see lyrics from their hit song "We are the Champions."

The background text is contents from a Wikipedia page that gives background information about Queen. Ha!

Neapolitan - Graphic Treatment

by Elizabeth Vazquez

These are graphic treatments I made inspired by neapolitan ice cream. The colors of strawberry, vanilla, and chocolate, and soft shades of blue complement each other well.

The color scheme, images, and typefaces remained contant, but playing with the placement of the elements was key in portraying different aesthetics for this concept.

Some other subtle differences are the cropping of the images as texture, the change in color sequence to play around with unity, and rotated grids.

Owl - Image Trace

by Elizabeth Vazquez

This is an exercise that delves into image tracing and uses "The Garden of Earthly Delights". The subject I chose to focus on from the paiting is an owl.

Some of the image traces stay true to the owl's portrayal in the painting and others are pieces that I wanted to make look different.

The latter of the image traces are the most intriguing because they utilize a variety of effects and/or visual elements to make them look ludicrous, insane, and zany.

Jacobs Building Redesign

by Elizabeth Vazquez, Alex Duan, and Malek Gallichio

This is a Housing & Innovation in Revit (HIR) project I designed for an engineering course.

The objective was to redesign the old Jacobs Building into a college campus expansion for NYU Tandon with the given specifications.

Our group name was MAE Enterprise and our slogan was: You MAE rest assured when MAE is on the job!

I was in change of handling the majority of designs that went into this project including sketches, the final building prototype, and our group logo.
